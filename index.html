About

"When the world continues to change, how do we avoid losing ourselves?"

Moving Image AI Narrative Immersive Environment Public Intervention Interdisciplinary Research Embodied Perception

Nerissa Mengyi Li is an interdisciplinary artist, independent curator, and art strategy researcher working between Shenzhen and London. Her practice begins with a persistent question: as the world continues to change, how do we remain ourselves?

Over the past several years her work has spanned public art, cultural strategy, urban regeneration, educational initiatives, and contemporary artistic creation. Through collaboration with cities, institutions, communities, and cultural systems, she has become increasingly interested in how technological transformation reshapes the ways we understand the world, others, and ourselves.

Rather than focusing on AI as a technological phenomenon, her research investigates a deeper human question: as more and more human capacities are mediated by external intelligent systems, how do we continue becoming ourselves?

Research

Two interconnected projects form two stages of the same inquiry: one reveals the risks of seeking meaning entirely outside ourselves; the other explores how we might return to ourselves.

I
ID-T7.16
AI · Intimacy · Identity
A sci-fi moving-image work set on Planet N31, where a preserved heart recounts how the pursuit of perfect love led to humanity's demise. Companion robots fulfill desire yet erode real emotion. When love is programmable and death circumvented — what defines us? The work examines future forms of intimacy shaped by AI, asking whether increasing technological companionship encourages people to seek meaning through external systems rather than within themselves.
2025 · Times Square, New York · Venice International Art Fair · Louvre, Paris
II
Kan Shan Shui
看山水 · Seeing the Landscape
Drawing from the classical Chinese philosophical progression — the mountain is a mountain; the mountain is no longer a mountain; the mountain is once again a mountain — this work explores how encounters with the world can become a process of self-recognition and the formation of an inner anchor. Centered on the eye-hand motif, it reconnects seeing with touch and consciousness with the body, constructing a posthuman surreal landscape in which the body is a medium for perceiving the world, not an object to be viewed.
2025–2026 · Digital moving image · 2D illustration series · Limited edition
